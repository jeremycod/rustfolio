# Rustfolio Tutorial – Accounts Page

> Audience: Beginners who want to understand how individual brokerage accounts are tracked  
> Goal: Learn how Rustfolio separates accounts, tracks performance, and analyzes risk at the account level.

---

# 🏦 What Is the Accounts Page?

The **Accounts** page lets you:

- View each brokerage account separately (e.g., RRSP, TFSA, Personal)
- See performance per account
- Track deposits and withdrawals
- Analyze risk per account
- Review transactions
- Inspect historical performance

If the Dashboard shows the *big picture*,  
the Accounts page shows the *individual building blocks*.

---

# 🧭 Page Overview

At the top:

- **My Portfolio selector**
- Import CSV
- Refresh

Below that:

- Consolidated Summary
- Individual Account cards
- Account details view (when selected)

---

# 📊 Consolidated Summary (All Accounts Combined)

Example metrics shown:

- Total Book Value
- Total Market Value
- Total Gain/Loss

### What These Mean

**Book Value**
= Total amount you invested (cost basis)

**Market Value**
= What everything is worth now

**Gain/Loss**
= Profit or loss across all accounts combined

This section answers:
> "How are all my accounts doing together?"

# 📊 Risk Analysis Tab

When switching to **Risk Analysis**, you see performance metrics.

Examples shown:

- Systematic Risk
- Unsystematic Risk
- Variance
- Sharpe Ratio
- Sortino Ratio
- Alpha
- Beta
- Max Drawdown
- Expected Return

Let's simplify:

### Beta
Measures how sensitive your account is to the overall market.

- Beta = 1 → moves with market
- Beta > 1 → more volatile than market
- Beta < 1 → less volatile

### Sharpe Ratio
Return per unit of risk.
Higher = better risk efficiency.

### Max Drawdown
Largest drop from peak value.

Important for understanding:
> "How bad could things get?"

# 🧠 Why Separate Accounts Matter

Different accounts serve different purposes:

- RRSP → retirement savings (long-term)
- Personal account → flexible investing
- TFSA → tax-efficient growth

Risk levels should often differ by account purpose.

Example:
- Retirement account = more diversified
- Trading account = possibly more aggressive

# ⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

❌ Looking only at total portfolio and ignoring account-level risk  
❌ Forgetting to track withdrawals  
❌ Comparing accounts without considering deposits  
❌ Ignoring benchmark comparison

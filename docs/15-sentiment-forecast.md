# Rustfolio Tutorial – Sentiment-Aware Forecasting

> Audience: Beginners who want to incorporate market sentiment  
> Goal: Incorporate news and insider activity into forecasts.

---

# 📰 What Is Sentiment-Aware Forecasting?

Traditional forecasting uses:
- Historical prices
- Technical indicators
- Statistical models

Sentiment-aware forecasting adds:
- News sentiment
- SEC filings
- Insider activity
- Market perception

---

# 🔍 Sentiment Factors

## News Sentiment

Analyzes:
- Article tone (positive/negative)
- Media coverage frequency
- Headline sentiment
- Analyst commentary

Example:

```
News Sentiment: 7.2/10 (Positive)
```

---

## SEC Filings

Tracks:
- Material events
- Corporate disclosures
- Regulatory filings
- Official announcements

Example:

```
Recent Filing: 8-K (Material Event)
Sentiment Impact: Neutral
```

---

## Insider Activity

Monitors:
- Insider buying
- Insider selling
- Transaction size
- Timing patterns

Example:

```
Recent Insider Buying: 3 transactions
Sentiment Impact: Positive
```

---

## Combined Sentiment Score

Aggregates all factors:

Example:

```
Combined Score: 8.8/10
Confidence: Medium
```

Higher score = more positive sentiment.

---

# 🧠 Why Sentiment Matters

Market moves are often driven by:

- Perception
- Headlines
- Institutional behavior
- Fear and greed

Sentiment forecasting captures that dimension.

---

# 📊 Sentiment + Price Forecast

Traditional forecast:

```
Price Target: $150
```

Sentiment-adjusted forecast:

```
Price Target: $155 (positive sentiment boost)
```

Sentiment can amplify or dampen price movements.

---

# 📈 Sentiment Divergence

When sentiment and price disagree:

Positive sentiment + Falling price:
May indicate buying opportunity

Negative sentiment + Rising price:
May indicate overvaluation

---

# ⚠️ Important Limitations

Sentiment is subjective and can be:
- Manipulated
- Lagging
- Overly reactive
- Herd-driven

Use as one input, not sole decision factor.

# ⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

❌ Following sentiment blindly  
❌ Ignoring fundamental analysis  
❌ Overreacting to single news items  
❌ Confusing sentiment with value  
❌ Not considering sentiment lag

Technical Signals:
> "What patterns suggest"

Price forecasts tell you:

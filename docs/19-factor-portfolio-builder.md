# Rustfolio Tutorial – Factor Portfolio Builder

> Audience: Investors who want to build systematic, data-driven portfolios  
> Goal: Construct a portfolio based on academic investment factors instead of individual stock picking.

---

# 🧠 What Is Factor Investing?

Factor investing builds portfolios using measurable characteristics that historically drive returns.

Instead of asking:

> "Which stock should I buy?"

You ask:

> "Which characteristics should I overweight?"

---

# 📊 Available Investment Factors

The tool allows you to select and weight:

## 🔵 Value
Stocks trading below intrinsic value.

Examples:
- Low P/E ratio
- Low price-to-book
- Discounted cash flow potential

Value stocks historically outperform over long periods.

---

## 🟢 Growth
Companies with strong revenue and earnings expansion.

Characteristics:
- High earnings growth
- Revenue acceleration
- Market share gains

Growth stocks drive portfolio appreciation.

---

## 🟠 Momentum
Stocks with strong recent price performance.

Concept:
Winners tend to keep winning (in the medium term).

Momentum captures trend-following behavior.

---

## 🟣 Quality
Companies with:
- Strong balance sheets
- Consistent earnings
- High return on equity
- Low debt

Quality provides stability and resilience.

---

## 🔷 Low Volatility
Stocks with historically smaller price swings.

Benefits:
- Reduced drawdowns
- Smoother returns
- Defensive characteristics

Low volatility anomaly: lower risk, similar returns.

---

# ⚖️ Adjusting Factor Weights

You can adjust sliders to define how much each factor matters.

Example shown:

```

Growth: 11%
Momentum: 30%
Quality: 10%

```

Weights are normalized automatically to total 100%.

# 🔄 Factor Rotation

Different factors perform better in different market conditions:

Bull Market:
- Momentum
- Growth

Bear Market:
- Quality
- Low Volatility

Recovery:
- Value
- Momentum

Understanding cycles helps with rebalancing.

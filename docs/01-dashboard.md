# Rustfolio Tutorial – Dashboard Overview

> Audience: Beginners with little or no trading experience  
> Goal: Understand what your portfolio contains, how it is performing, and how risky it is — all from one page.

---

# 🧭 What Is the Dashboard?

The **Dashboard** is your portfolio command center.

It gives you a quick summary of:

- 💰 How much your portfolio is worth
- 📈 How much you've gained or lost
- ⚠️ How risky your portfolio is
- 🧠 What's driving your risk
- 📰 What news might impact your holdings
- 🤖 AI-generated insights and recommendations

If you're new to investing, this page answers the most important question:

> "How am I doing right now — and should I be worried?"

---

# 🏠 Top Summary Cards (At the Top of the Page)

## 1️⃣ Total Portfolio Value

**Example shown:** `$414,560.76`

This is the current total market value of all your investments combined.

It includes:
- Stocks
- ETFs
- Cash (if tracked)
- Any other assets in your portfolio

📌 Important:
This number changes daily as market prices move.

---

## 2️⃣ Total Deposits

**Example shown:** `$399,734.61`

This is how much money you originally invested (including additional contributions).

This helps you compare:

- Deposits = what you put in  
- Portfolio Value = what it's worth now  

---

## 3️⃣ True Gain/Loss

**Example shown:** `+$14,826.15`

This shows your actual profit or loss.

Formula (simplified):

```
Portfolio Value – Total Deposits = True Gain/Loss
```

If positive → you're up  
If negative → you're down  

---

## 4️⃣ Accounts

Shows how many brokerage accounts are connected.

Example: `6`

Useful if you:
- Combine retirement + trading accounts
- Track multiple investment accounts

# 📊 Portfolio Risk & Performance Metrics

## Portfolio Risk Score

**Example shown:** `47 (moderate)`

Scale: 1–100  
- Low = conservative  
- High = aggressive  

This tells you how volatile your portfolio is.

👉 Beginners should aim for a moderate score unless they fully understand high volatility.

---

## Portfolio Volatility

**Example shown:** `25389.09% Annualized`  
(This appears unusually high in the demo, but volatility normally reflects price movement intensity.)

Volatility measures:
> How dramatically your portfolio value moves up and down.

High volatility = larger swings  
Low volatility = more stable

---

## Sharpe Ratio

**Example shown:** `0.65`

The Sharpe ratio measures:
> How much return you get per unit of risk.

Higher is better.

Rough interpretation:
- Below 1 = moderate efficiency
- Above 1 = good
- Above 2 = very strong

---

## News Sentiment

Shows overall sentiment:
- 🟢 Positive
- 🟡 Neutral
- 🔴 Negative

This is AI-analyzed market news affecting your holdings.

# 🔥 Top Risk Contributors

This section lists stocks that contribute most to your portfolio risk.

Example symbols shown:
- HOOD
- CRCL
- META
- AMZN
- COCO

Why this matters:

Even if a stock is profitable, it can increase overall portfolio risk.

If one stock dominates this list repeatedly:
- You may be overexposed
- Diversification might help

# 🏆 Top Holdings

This table shows your largest positions.

Columns include:
- Ticker
- Value
- Weight (% of portfolio)
- Risk indicator
- Volatility

### What "Weight" Means

If a stock has 7% weight:
It represents 7% of your total portfolio.

If one stock exceeds ~15–20%:
You may be heavily concentrated.

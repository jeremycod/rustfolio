# Rustfolio Tutorial – CVaR (Tail-Risk) Analysis

> Audience: Beginners who want to understand worst-case scenarios  
> Goal: Understand worst-case loss scenarios beyond normal volatility.

---

# 📉 What Is CVaR?

CVaR = Conditional Value at Risk  
Also called Expected Shortfall.

It answers:

> "If things go really badly, how bad could losses be?"

Unlike normal volatility, CVaR focuses only on extreme downside events.

---

# 📊 Portfolio Tail-Risk Summary

You'll see:

- Value at Risk (95% confidence)
- Expected Shortfall (95% confidence)
- Same metrics at 99%

## Example Interpretation

VaR (95%) = -0.76%  
Means: There's a 5% chance portfolio loses more than 0.76% in the given period.

Expected Shortfall (95%) = -3.84%  
Means: If that worst 5% scenario happens, average loss is 3.84%.

---

# 🧠 Why CVaR Matters

Normal volatility underestimates crashes.

CVaR measures:

- Black swan exposure
- Extreme downside vulnerability
- Stress scenario risk

---

# 📈 95% vs 99% Confidence

95% confidence:
Captures 1-in-20 bad days

99% confidence:
Captures 1-in-100 worst days

Higher confidence = more extreme scenarios.

---

# ⚠️ When To Use CVaR

- During high market uncertainty
- When managing retirement portfolios
- When limiting downside is priority
- Before major market events
- When stress-testing portfolio

---

# 🧩 CVaR vs Standard Deviation

Standard Deviation:
Measures all volatility (up and down)

CVaR:
Measures only extreme losses

CVaR is more relevant for risk-averse investors.

---

# 📌 How Beginners Should Use This Page

Step 1:
Check Expected Shortfall at 95%

Step 2:
Compare to your loss tolerance

Step 3:
If too high, reduce high-risk positions

Step 4:
Monitor during volatile periods

---

# ✅ What This Page Helps You Answer

- What's my worst-case loss?
- Am I exposed to tail risk?
- How bad could a crash be?
- Should I hedge extreme downside?

---

# 📌 Final Takeaway

Volatility tells you:
> "How much does my portfolio move?"

CVaR tells you:
> "How bad could the worst days be?"

Both are essential for complete risk understanding.

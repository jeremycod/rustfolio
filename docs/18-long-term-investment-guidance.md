# Rustfolio Tutorial – Long-Term Investment Guidance

> Audience: Beginners building a long-term investment strategy  
> Goal: Generate a personalized portfolio allocation based on goals, time horizon, and risk tolerance.

---

# 🎯 What Is Long-Term Investment Guidance?

This tool answers:

> "Given my goals and risk tolerance, how should I structure my portfolio?"

It's a guided process that builds:

- Asset allocation recommendations  
- Risk distribution  
- Suggested holdings  
- Long-term growth strategy  

Think of it as a simplified robo-advisor built into Rustfolio.

---

# 🧭 Step 1 – Select Investment Goal

Options shown:

- Retirement  
- College Fund  
- Wealth Building  

Each goal influences:

- Risk profile  
- Asset allocation  
- Growth vs stability balance  

Example:

Retirement → balanced growth & preservation  
Wealth Building → higher growth focus  

---

# ⏳ Step 2 – Time Horizon

You select how long you plan to invest:

Examples shown:
- 10 Years  
- 14 Years  

Longer time horizon:
More growth-oriented allocation possible.

Shorter time horizon:
More conservative allocation recommended.

---

# ⚖️ Step 3 – Risk Tolerance

Options:

- Conservative  
- Moderate  
- Aggressive  

## Conservative
Focus on capital preservation.  
Lower volatility.

## Moderate
Balanced growth & stability.  
Most common choice.

## Aggressive
Higher growth potential.  
Higher volatility and drawdowns.

---

# 📊 Step 4 – Recommended Asset Allocation

Example outputs:

```
Low Risk: 12%  
Medium Risk: 35%  
High Risk: 53%
```

Allocation adjusts dynamically based on:

- Time horizon
- Risk tolerance
- Investment goal

This shows how your capital should be distributed across risk tiers.

---

# 📦 Step 5 – Recommended Holdings

The system suggests specific stocks or ETFs.

Each recommendation includes:

- Risk Level
- Sector
- Quality Score
- Growth Score
- Value Score
- Dividend Score
- Overall Score

# 🧠 How It Works Conceptually

The tool balances:

Risk Capacity (time horizon)  
Risk Tolerance (comfort level)  
Investment Objective  

Then maps that to:

Growth vs Stability weighting.

---

# 📌 Practical Example

If:

```
Goal: Wealth Building  
Horizon: 14 Years  
Risk: Aggressive  
```

The model may recommend:

- High equity exposure
- More growth stocks
- Lower bond allocation

If:

```
Goal: Retirement  
Horizon: 5 Years  
Risk: Conservative  
```

The model shifts toward:

- Defensive stocks
- Lower volatility assets
- Reduced drawdown risk

# 📈 Understanding the Scores

## Quality Score
Financial health, profitability, stability

## Growth Score
Revenue and earnings growth potential

## Value Score
Valuation attractiveness (P/E, P/B ratios)

## Dividend Score
Dividend yield and consistency

## Overall Score
Weighted combination of all factors

Higher scores = better fit for your profile.

# 📊 Implementing the Recommendations

Don't rush:

1. Review all suggested holdings
2. Research each company
3. Check current risk metrics
4. Start with 1-2 positions
5. Build gradually over time
6. Rebalance quarterly

---

# 🎯 Aligning Current Portfolio

Compare:

Current allocation vs Recommended allocation

If misaligned:

- Identify overweight positions
- Identify underweight positions
- Plan gradual rebalancing
- Consider tax implications

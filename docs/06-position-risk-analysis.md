# Rustfolio Tutorial – Position Risk Analysis Page

> Audience: Beginners who want to understand the risk of a single stock  
> Goal: Learn how to evaluate volatility, drawdown, beta, and sentiment for an individual position.

---

# 🔍 What Is the Position Risk Analysis Page?

This page answers:

> "How risky is this specific stock in my portfolio?"

While the Portfolio Risk page looks at the entire portfolio,  
this section focuses on one ticker at a time.

Example shown in the video:

```
AAPL – Apple Inc.
```

---

# 🧭 Page Structure

Main components:

- Search Position Risk Metrics
- Risk Metrics Overview
- Risk Trends
- Risk History
- Sentiment Signals
- Enhanced Sentiment (AI-powered)

---

# 🔎 Search Position Risk Metrics

At the top, you can:

- Enter a ticker (e.g., AAPL)
- Select time period (e.g., 90 Days)
- Choose benchmark (e.g., S&P 500)
- Click Analyze

This allows comparison of the stock against a market index.

---

# 📊 AAPL Risk Analysis – Overview

Example shown:

```
Overall Risk Score: 73.8 (High)
```

Scale: 0–100  
Higher score = higher risk.

This score is calculated relative to the benchmark.

---

# 📉 Core Risk Metrics (Single Stock)

## Volatility

Example:

```
Current Volatility: 1761.28%
```

Volatility measures how much the price moves.

High volatility:
- Larger price swings
- Higher emotional stress
- Greater short-term risk

---

## Average Volatility

Used to compare:
Current volatility vs historical baseline.

If current > average:
Risk may be increasing.

---

## Current Drawdown

Example:

```
-7.03%
```

This means:
The stock is currently 7.03% below its recent peak.

---

## Maximum Drawdown

Example:

```
-87.58%
```

This shows the worst historical drop.

Important:
Stocks can recover — but large drawdowns show fragility.

---

# 📈 Risk Trends Tab

Shows rolling metrics over time:

- Volatility
- Drawdown
- Beta
- Sharpe Ratio
- Sortino Ratio

This helps answer:

> "Is risk increasing or decreasing?"

If volatility trend rises sharply:
The stock may be entering unstable conditions.

---

# 📊 Risk History Tab

Graph shows multiple metrics simultaneously:

- Risk Score
- Volatility
- Max Drawdown
- Beta
- Sharpe Ratio

This allows deeper analysis of:

- Stability
- Risk-adjusted performance
- Historical instability patterns

---

# 📣 Sentiment Signals Tab

This analyzes:

- News-based sentiment
- Media tone
- Institutional commentary

It attempts to measure:

> "Is the market feeling positive or negative about this stock?"

Sentiment can influence volatility.

---

# 🤖 Enhanced Sentiment (Experimental AI)

This is an advanced AI-driven feature.

It combines:

- News Sentiment
- SEC Filings
- Insider Activity

---

## Combined Sentiment Analysis

Example:

```
Score: 8.8
Confidence: Low
```

Lower confidence means:
Signal strength is weak or limited data available.

---

## News Sentiment

Measures:
- Tone of recent articles
- Frequency of positive vs negative language

---

## SEC Filings

Tracks:
- Material corporate events
- Official disclosures

---

## Insider Activity

Tracks:
- Buy transactions
- Sell transactions

Insider buying can indicate confidence.  
Insider selling can indicate caution — but context matters.

---

# ⚠️ Important Warning Shown

Enhanced Sentiment is marked as:

> Experimental

Meaning:
It should support decisions — not replace research.

# 📌 When To Use Position Risk Analysis

- Before buying a stock
- After large price moves
- When portfolio volatility increases
- When considering trimming a position

---

# ⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

❌ Ignoring volatility  
❌ Misinterpreting insider selling  
❌ Confusing sentiment with fundamentals  
❌ Focusing only on recent performance  

---

# 🧠 Risk vs Return Reminder

High-growth stocks often have:

- Higher volatility
- Larger drawdowns
- Greater emotional swings

Risk analysis helps determine if:

> "This stock fits my risk tolerance."

---

# ✅ What This Page Helps You Answer

- Is this stock high risk?
- Is volatility rising?
- How bad have past declines been?
- Is sentiment positive or negative?
- Is insider activity meaningful?

---

# 🔄 Portfolio Context

Even if a stock is high risk,
it may still fit your portfolio if:

- Position size is small
- Portfolio diversification is strong
- You accept volatility

Risk at position level ≠ bad investment.
It must be evaluated in context.

---

# 📌 Final Takeaway

Portfolio Risk Page:
> "How risky is my overall portfolio?"

Position Risk Analysis:
> "How risky is this specific stock?"

Both views are essential for informed decision-making.

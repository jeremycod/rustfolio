# Rustfolio Tutorial – Portfolio Page

> Audience: Beginners learning how to manage and optimize their investments  
> Goal: Understand portfolio health, risk concentration, and how to rebalance intelligently.

---

# 📊 What Is the Portfolio Page?

The **Portfolio** page answers a deeper question than the Dashboard:

> "Is my portfolio structured properly — or am I taking unnecessary risk?"

While the Dashboard summarizes performance,  
this page focuses on:

- Portfolio Health
- Risk Concentration
- Optimization Suggestions
- Aggregated Holdings
- Rebalancing Guidance

This is where portfolio management actually happens.

---

# 🔝 Top Summary Section

At the top you see familiar metrics:

- **Current Value**
- **Total Deposits**
- **Total Withdrawals**
- **True Gain/Loss**

These confirm performance before diving into structure and risk.

---

# 🟢 Portfolio Health Panel

Example shown:

```
Portfolio Health: GOOD
```

This section includes:

- Critical Issues
- High Priority Warnings
- Diversification Score
- Key Findings
- Refresh Analysis button

## What Portfolio Health Means

It's a high-level assessment of:

- Risk balance
- Concentration
- Diversification
- Structural weaknesses

If marked GOOD:
Your diversification and concentration are generally acceptable.

If marked WARNING:
There may be excessive exposure to certain positions.

---

# 📈 Current Portfolio Metrics

This area shows quick stats like:

- Risk Score
- Volatility
- Largest Position %
- Total Positions

These help beginners quickly understand:

- Is one stock too dominant?
- Is the portfolio too small (low diversification)?
- Is volatility too high?

# ⚠️ Optimization Recommendations

This is one of the most important sections.

Example shown:

```
High Concentration Risk in FIDS694
```

One position represents too large a portion of your portfolio.

---

## Affected Position Panel

You see:

- Ticker
- Action (e.g., SELL)
- Current Value
- Recommended Value
- Suggested Change

This helps answer:

> "How much should I reduce this position?"

---

# 📉 Why This Matters Section

This explains in plain language:

- How much of the portfolio the asset represents
- Why that is risky
- How it impacts volatility

Example concept:
Even if a stock is only 12% of holdings count,
it might contribute 40%+ of risk.

Risk contribution ≠ position size.

---

# 🧠 Suggested Actions

Examples shown:

- Reduce position by X%
- Consider replacing with lower volatility alternative
- Diversify into same sector alternatives

This is practical rebalancing guidance.

Important:
These are analytical suggestions, not financial advice.

# 📊 Aggregated Holdings

This section combines holdings across all accounts.

Columns include:

- Symbol
- Name
- Asset Type
- Quantity
- Market Value
- Gain/Loss
- Gain %
- Risk indicator

This differs from the Accounts page because:

Accounts page → per account  
Portfolio page → combined across all accounts  

---

# 🔎 How to Read Aggregated Holdings

## Gain/Loss
Shows total unrealized profit.

## Asset Type
Helps evaluate diversification:
- Too many mutual funds?
- No ETFs?
- Heavy single-stock exposure?

## Risk Indicator
Shows which positions are high volatility.

---

# 📉 Concentration Risk Explained (For Beginners)

Concentration risk happens when:

- One asset dominates portfolio value
- One sector dominates exposure
- One asset contributes most volatility

Even if profitable, concentration increases risk.

Example:
If 30% of your portfolio is in one stock,
a 10% drop in that stock = 3% portfolio drop instantly.

---

# 🔄 When Should You Use This Page?

Use it when:

- You suspect overexposure
- After large gains in one stock
- Before rebalancing
- During market volatility
- Reviewing long-term portfolio structure

---

# ⚠️ Common Beginner Mistakes

❌ Ignoring concentration because position is profitable  
❌ Confusing number of holdings with diversification  
❌ Thinking 10 stocks automatically means low risk  
❌ Never reviewing optimization suggestions  

---

# ✅ What This Page Helps You Answer

- Am I too concentrated?
- Which asset contributes most risk?
- Should I reduce a position?
- Am I diversified across asset types?
- Is my portfolio structurally healthy?

---

# 🧠 Beginner Rule of Thumb

If:
- One position > 20% of portfolio
- One sector > 40%
- Risk score very high

Consider rebalancing.

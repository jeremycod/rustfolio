# Rustfolio Tutorial – Stock Screener

> Audience: Beginners who want to discover new stocks  
> Goal: Discover stocks using quantitative filters.

---

# 🔍 What Is the Stock Screener?

The Stock Screener helps you find stocks matching specific criteria.

Instead of randomly searching,
you filter by:
- Fundamentals
- Valuation
- Technical indicators
- Risk metrics

---

# 📊 Factor Selection

Categories:

## Fundamentals
- Earnings growth
- Revenue growth
- Profit margins
- Return on equity (ROE)

## Valuation
- P/E Ratio
- Price-to-Book
- Price-to-Sales
- Dividend Yield

## Technical
- Price momentum
- Moving averages
- RSI (Relative Strength Index)
- Volume trends

## Risk
- Beta
- Volatility
- Max Drawdown
- Sharpe Ratio

---

# 🔧 Common Filters

## P/E Ratio

Example: P/E < 20

Finds stocks trading at lower valuations.

---

## Beta

Example: Beta < 1.2

Finds stocks with moderate market sensitivity.

---

## Earnings Growth

Example: Growth > 15%

Finds companies with strong earnings expansion.

---

## Dividend Yield

Example: Yield > 3%

Finds income-generating stocks.

---

## Debt to Equity

Example: D/E < 0.5

Finds companies with low debt.

---

## Market Cap

Example: Market Cap > $10B

Filters by company size:
- Large cap (> $10B)
- Mid cap ($2B - $10B)
- Small cap (< $2B)

---

## Price Range

Example: $50 - $200

Filters by absolute price.

---

# 🧠 How Beginners Should Use It

Step 1:
Choose risk profile (Conservative / Moderate / Aggressive)

Step 2:
Apply 2–3 filters max

Step 3:
Review results with Risk Analysis

Step 4:
Research individual stocks

Step 5:
Never buy based on screening alone

---

# ⚠️ Avoid Over-Filtering

Too many filters = no results.

Start broad, then narrow down.

Example progression:

1. Market Cap > $5B
2. Add: P/E < 25
3. Add: Beta < 1.5
4. Review results
5. Refine if needed

---

# 📊 Screening Strategies

## Value Investing
- Low P/E
- Low P/B
- High dividend yield
- Low debt

## Growth Investing
- High earnings growth
- High revenue growth
- Higher P/E acceptable
- Strong momentum

## Dividend Investing
- Dividend yield > 3%
- Consistent dividend history
- Low payout ratio
- Stable earnings

## Low Volatility
- Beta < 1
- Low volatility
- Defensive sectors
- Stable earnings
